**Ecosystem:** Used by Roam, Logseq, Athens, and many knowledge tools.

**Our differentiator:** We didn't set out to build a Roam clone — we
set out (a decade earlier) to build a Xanadu "clone." Arxana's
scholia-based model, hyperedge structure, and annotation-as-entity
approach is structurally different from the note-taking paradigm.

**Market analysis needed:** Why is WebArxana different from Roam/Logseq?

- Hyperedges link multiple entities with roles (not just bidirectional)

- Multi-focus canvas with d3-force layout (not outliner)
- The graph accumulates — new sessions start from the full structure

**This is presumably worth something on the market.** The question is
what market and what form — SaaS? Open-source with consulting?
Enterprise knowledge management? Research collaboration tooling?
